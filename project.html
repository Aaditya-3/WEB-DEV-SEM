About ISRO


Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of India. The prime objective of ISRO is the development and application of space technology for various national needs. Alongside its technological advancement, ISRO contributes to science and science education in the country.

Charting the Cosmos: Our Vision and Mission

  • Harness, sustain and augment space technology for national development, while pursuing space science research and planetary exploration.
  • Design and development of launch vehicles and related technologies for providing access to space.
  • Satellite-based Remote Sensing programme for management of natural resources and monitoring of environment using space-based imagery.
  • Research and Development in space science and technology for the nation.
  • Promote and authorise private firms to play key roles in the global space market.




TIMELINE OF ISRO








Establishment

ISRO was established on August 15, 1969 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai. This laid the foundation for India’s journey into space exploration, focusing on harnessing space technology for societal benefit.

Aryabhata

India’s first satellite, Aryabhata, was launched into space on April 19, 1975 from the Soviet Union. The satellite was a symbol of India's scientific aspirations on a global platform.

SLV-3

ISRO successfully launched the Rohini-1 satellite into orbit on July 18, 1980 using the Satellite Launch Vehicle 3. This achievement marked India’s first successful satellite launch using an indigenously built rocket.

Chandrayaan-1

ISRO launched Chandrayaan-1, India’s first lunar exploration mission on October 22, 2008. This mission made a groundbreaking discovery by detecting water molecules on the Moon’s surface.

Mangalyaan

ISRO launched Mangalyaan on November 5, 2013, its Mars Orbiter Mission, which entered Mars’ orbit on September 24, 2014. India became the first nation to reach Mars on its maiden attempt.

Chandrayaan-3

ISRO successfully landed the Vikram Lander of the Chandrayaan-3 mission on the Moon’s south pole. India became the first country to achieve this milestone and the fourth country ever to soft-land on the lunar surface.
